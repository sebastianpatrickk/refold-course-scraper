Course overview

Here at Refold, we're not here to teach you a language. Our goal is to teach you how to learn any language on your own, wherever and whenever you want to, by combining media immersion, strategic study, and the best tools and techniques out there.

Here are some of the general topics that we will be covering in this course:

  • Refold philosophy and language acquisition

  • The basics of how to read and listen to your language

  • How to learn vocabulary and grammar so well that it becomes instinctual

  • The importance of consistency and habits

  • The many techniques for understanding media content

  • The most helpful tools for learning a language

  • How to find the best media content for you

We've also created a database of useful instructional materials and media content in all the major languages so that you don't need to waste time searching for the right thing to use. You can find a link to this database on the community Discord, or in the next module.

The structure of a lesson in this course

Every lesson is presented by your coach, Ben. Ben will introduce some new information, explain why that information is important and how to take advantage of it, and finally, give you a specific assignment to do in order to cement this knowledge.

How to succeed in this course

This is a long course. 30 total days. We made it that length because we think it's vitally important to learn every single day and develop good habits around your learning. Plus, there's a lot to cover!

The completion rate of online courses, across the whole internet, is very low. Anywhere from 5-15%. We want to make sure as many of you as possible achieve your dreams of fluency, which is why this lesson is all about "Creating your learning sanctuary."

Nothing is better at stopping you from learning than distractions.

Distractions can hinder the learning process and make it difficult to concentrate. It is essential to have a safe learning environment free from distractions to enhance productivity and maximize learning.

Common distractions in the learning environment:

  • Noise, social media, TV, phone calls, etc.

  • All of these distractions mess with your attention span and make it hard to focus. Trying to learn new things without being focused usually results in bad results.

Practical tips for creating a safe learning environment:

  • Set up a designated study area in your house that is a different place from your usual relaxation area.

  • Turn off the phone or keep it on silent

  • Avoid social media

  • Let other people you're living with know that you will be busy and not to disturb you

Creating a safe learning environment free from distractions is crucial for effective learning. By following the practical tips discussed in this lesson, you can create a safe learning environment that will help you achieve your academic and personal goals.

The other critical decision that will help you complete the course is to set a time. Being specific with your time makes it much, much easier to learn every day. Choosing a consistent time to engage with the lessons of a course is important for establishing a routine, maximizing focus and attention, and enhancing retention of the material.

Consider your schedule and preferences, and then choose a time. It doesn't have to be the same each day (weekends might be different, for example), but there should be purpose behind your decision.

And then, you need to be consistent, and prioritize learning. Having a set time for learning each day can help you build habits and stick with them over time, train your brain to be primed for learning, and solidify the material in your memory.

Each lesson will take around 20 minutes to complete. But, you'll also need to spend time with your language each day, so block off another 30–45 minutes each day for that.

Once you've chosen your times, put them into your calendar and set a reminder if you think you might need one.

Day 2: Refold Philosophy

Welcome back, everyone! Today we’re going over how and why our philosophy is different from conventional learning methods.

In traditional methods, language learning often resembles solving math equations. They’re focused on memorization and drilling grammar rules so you can fill in the blanks.

The conventional approach will not make you fluent.

At Refold, our focus lies in fostering a natural language instinct. Rather than fixating on rigid rules and equations, our goal is to show you how to immerse in the language and develop a deep understanding. Immersion is the best way to build an intuitive grasp of the language, paving the way for effortless speaking.

There are no shortcuts to fluency, but if you spend enough time in the language, you’ll not only develop an instinct for the language but learning it will be effortless — because you’ll be having fun.

Day 9: Reading + Listening

Welcome to day 9! Today’s lesson is about how to combine reading and listening.

As we discussed in lesson 1, reading is one of the most powerful tools we have, as learners, to acquire vocabulary in our target language. When reading, you can see each individual word, how they're connected to other words, and look up anything you don’t know.

However, when the sounds of your new language are still hard to hear, you can’t connect the sounds of the language to the words you’re reading on the page.

That’s why we recommend starting out by reading and listening at the same time. We call this ‘dual channel reading.”

Ben will walk you through different ways to leverage this technique in your language-learning routine.

You're NOT stupid. Not understanding is normal. It's something most language learners need to overcome, and we have good news. There are many strategies you can use to mitigate the discomfort of ambiguity. Ben will show you how to apply them to your own study routine.

Day 15: What is Acquisition?

Welcome to the first day of week 3! Today we'll learn about acquisition, understand what it means, and why we should acquire languages instead of learn them.

In earlier lessons, we discussed the idea of developing an instinct for a language. To develop a natural instinct, you need to acquire a language instead of learn it.

Learning a language implies memorization, but memorization is fragile. Have you ever crammed for a test and then forgot everything you learned within a week? The same principle applies here. When you acquire a language, you can’t remember ever not knowing it. It’s so ingrained in your memory it feels like it’s been there forever.

Listen in as Ben goes into even further detail, explains the benefits of acquisition over memorization, and helps you understand what this means for your own personal language journey.

Day 16: From Zero to Fluency

Hey there, language learners! Today, we're diving into the process of achieving fluency.

Here at Refold, we recommend a three-stage approach in the following order: reading, listening, and then output.

During the reading phase, your objective is to expand your vocabulary and grammar knowledge. The listening phase focuses on training your ability to effortlessly follow native conversations at their natural pace without conscious effort. Finally, the output phase is where you'll put your understanding into practice and develop fluency in speaking.

In this session, Ben will walk you through the specific goals of each phase and shed light on the challenges and requirements for progressing to the next stage.

Day 18: Interactive Immersion

Day 22: Freeflow

In today's lesson, we'll explore the power of freeflow immersion .

Freeflow immersion allows you to relax and enjoy the content while staying attentive. It not only enhances self-sufficiency by relying on your subconscious understanding, but also prepares you for real-world situations where you won't have your language tools readily available.

Moreover, one of the great advantages of freeflow immersion is that it provides up to five times more exposure to the language. However, if you're not engaged with what you're immersing with, you won't gain anything from it. That's why it's essential to choose content that aligns with your interests and skill level.

In this lesson, Ben will help you discover the best content and strategies to maximize your freeflow immersion experience.
